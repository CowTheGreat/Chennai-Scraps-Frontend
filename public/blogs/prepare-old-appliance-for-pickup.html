Simple Guide: How to Prepare Any Old Appliance for Pickup

A quick prep checklist that makes pickup faster, safer, and smoother.

Selling an old appliance is only half the job. Preparing it correctly saves time, avoids damage, and makes the process easy for you and the pickup team.

Whether you are selling an AC, fridge, washing machine, or microwave, these simple steps help.

Step 1: Disconnect safely

  • Turn off the main power switch.
  • Unplug the power cable.
  • For washers and dishwashers, close the water tap and remove hoses.
  • If gas or fixed lines are involved, use a professional for uninstallation.

Step 2: Empty and clean the appliance

Appliance Quick Preparation
Refrigerator Remove food and trays, defrost if needed
Washing machine Empty drum, remove detergent and trapped items
Microwave or oven Take out trays, containers, and plates
AC (after uninstall) Keep remote separately and wipe visible dust

Step 3: Keep accessories together

  • Fridge: ice trays and removable shelves.
  • Washing machine: inlet hose, outlet pipe, manual if available.
  • AC: remote, stabilizer if sold together, and removed brackets.

Place all accessories in one bag or box so nothing gets misplaced.

Step 4: Clear the path for movement

  • Create a clear route from appliance to door.
  • Move stools, side tables, and small obstacles.
  • Inform the team early if your floor has no lift.

Step 5: Be ready for quick verification

At pickup, the team may visually verify condition, confirm the details you shared, and validate payment details. If everything matches, collection is usually very quick.
